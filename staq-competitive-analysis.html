Feature Recommendations for Staq
Prioritized based on competitive gaps, user demand signals, and differentiation potential
Must Have ‚Äî V1 Launch

Bulletproof Share Extension

Save-to-stack in ‚â§2 taps from any app. Option to pick stack OR auto-assign. This is THE feature that makes or breaks every app in this space. Saver and PickTok prove it.

Signal: Every top-reviewed app cites the share extension as #1 feature
Must Have ‚Äî V1 Launch

Rich Link Previews

Every saved link shows thumbnail, title, source platform icon, and a 1-line description. Without this, saves feel like a list of URLs. Every competitor has this.

Signal: Saver's addition of link previews is mentioned in multiple positive reviews
Must Have ‚Äî V1 Launch

Content Persistence

Cache thumbnails and key metadata locally so saves remain useful even if the original post is deleted. Users mention this fear constantly ‚Äî "creators delete posts" is a real pain.

Signal: Dewey, Faves, MyMind all market this. Users cite it in reviews.
Must Have ‚Äî V1 Launch

AI Auto-Categorization + Manual Override

Your confidence-tier system (auto/maybe/inbox) is the unique middle ground between MyMind's "organize nothing" and Saver's "organize everything manually." Ship this in V1 ‚Äî it's your key differentiator.

Signal: No competitor has this. Trott auto-sorts but has no user override. Saver is 100% manual.
Must Have ‚Äî V1 Launch

Content-Type Detection (at minimum)

Identify whether a save is a recipe, product, place, article, video, etc. Display a type badge. Even without full enrichment, this context makes the grid useful.

Signal: Trott and Trunky both lead with this. MyMind does it silently.
Must Have ‚Äî V1 Launch

Search Across All Saves

Keyword search across titles, tags, notes, and AI-generated metadata. Users with 200+ saves need this immediately.

Signal: MyMind's #1 praised feature. Saver's biggest missing feature per reviews.

Staq's Unique Advantages (Features Nobody Else Has)
These are already in your design that no competitor offers

Confidence-Tier Organization

High confidence ‚Üí auto-placed. Medium ‚Üí "Maybe Recipes?" suggestion. Low ‚Üí Inbox triage. User corrections train the model. Nobody else has this middle ground between full-auto (MyMind/Trott) and full-manual (Saver/PickTok).

Learning Loop from Corrections

When a user moves something from "Products" to "Gift Ideas," Staq learns. Trott and Trunky auto-sort but never learn from user behavior. This is a genuine ML moat that improves with usage.

Universal Content Types

Trott does video only. Trunky does food/travel/movies only. Saver does everything but dumbly. Staq handles ALL content types (links, videos, articles, products, places) with smart enrichment per type.

The "Stacks" Metaphor

Not folders (boring), not boards (Pinterest), not collections (generic), not "spaces" (MyMind). Stacks feel tactile, stackable, browsable. Combined with the Q in Staq ‚Äî ownable brand language.

The Bottom Line
Strategic Summary

The market is moving fast ‚Äî Trott and Trunky appeared in the last 6 months with nearly identical theses to Staq. But both are early, small, and have critical gaps. The window is open but narrowing.


V1 priority order: (1) Nail the share extension. Saver proves this alone drives love. (2) Ship AI auto-categorization with confidence tiers ‚Äî your unique differentiator. (3) Rich previews + content-type badges. (4) Search.


V1.1 priority: Recipe enrichment first (highest demand), then travel/maps, then video summaries, then AI chat.


V2 differentiation: Serendipity mode, shared stacks, grocery lists, widgets, screenshot import. These are the features that turn a utility app into a beloved daily-open app.
