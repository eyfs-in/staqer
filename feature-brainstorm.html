Part 1: The Platform Question

Let me give you the honest, nuanced answer ‚Äî not just "go iOS." There are three realistic paths, each with real tradeoffs:

‚ú¶ RECOMMENDED

Path A: Native iOS First ‚Üí Android Later

  • ‚úì Foundation Models is Swift-native ‚Äî no bridging
  • ‚úì Share Extensions work reliably in native
  • ‚úì Apple actively promotes apps using Foundation Models ‚Äî editorial feature potential
  • ‚úì SwiftUI + Liquid Glass = premium feel
  • ‚úì iOS users 2x more likely to pay for apps
  • ‚úì Fastest path to a polished MVP
  • ‚úó No Android at launch
  • ‚úó Need to learn Swift/SwiftUI (new for you)
  • ‚úó Android later = separate codebase
PRACTICAL ALTERNATIVE

Path B: Flutter UI + Native AI Channels

  • ‚úì Leverages your Flutter expertise
  • ‚úì Cross-platform UI from day one
  • ‚úì Shared business logic and data layer
  • ‚úó Still need Swift platform channel for Foundation Models
  • ‚úó Still need Kotlin channel for ML Kit GenAI
  • ‚úó Share Extensions in Flutter are fragile and limited
  • ‚úó ~30-40% extra work on bridging layer
  • ‚úó Can't use Liquid Glass / Material You natively
  • ‚úó Apple less likely to feature Flutter apps
AMBITIOUS

Path C: Native iOS + Native Android

  • ‚úì Best possible experience on both platforms
  • ‚úì Full native AI access everywhere
  • ‚úì Both App Store and Play Store from launch
  • ‚úó Double the development effort
  • ‚úó Need proficiency in both Swift and Kotlin
  • ‚úó Feature parity becomes a constant struggle
  • ‚úó Only viable with a team or co-founder

My Recommendation: Path A ‚Äî Native iOS First, Validate, Then Expand

Here's why this isn't just "iOS only" ‚Äî it's a staged strategy:

For this specific app, the on-device AI IS the product. Apple's Foundation Models framework is deeply tied to Swift's type system (@Generable macros, guided generation, tool calling). Building this in Flutter would mean writing all the AI logic in Swift anyway, then bridging it ‚Äî adding complexity without adding value. The share extension (your #1 entry point) is also significantly more reliable in native iOS.

More importantly: Apple is actively looking for apps that showcase Foundation Models. They highlighted SmartGym, Stoic, Day One, OmniFocus in press releases within weeks of iOS 26 launch. A well-built AI curation app could earn editorial featuring ‚Äî which is worth more than any marketing budget.

The plan: Launch iOS ‚Üí validate product-market fit (3-4 months) ‚Üí if traction is strong, build Android natively with Kotlin + ML Kit GenAI APIs. Shared backend (Supabase or Firebase) keeps data portable. By then, Gemini Nano's Prompt API will be out of alpha and into production.

Your Flutter skills aren't wasted. If you later want a web companion, admin dashboard, or decide Android needs to ship fast ‚Äî Flutter is perfect for those. And your understanding of cross-platform architecture will make the backend design cleaner.

The one thing to remember:

The MVP is deceptively simple for the user ‚Äî share a link, it appears in the right bucket, done. But behind that simplicity is a powerful AI pipeline running entirely on their device. That contrast between effortless UX and sophisticated intelligence is the entire product thesis. Every feature should reinforce it: "I just saved it and it already knew exactly what it was."
